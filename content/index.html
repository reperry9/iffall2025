Try these YUMMY Chocolate Chip Pancakes from Allrecipes!

Estimated time:

30 minutes (10 minuts preperation, 20 minutes cooking)

Yeild:

Approx. 6 pancakes

The best chocolate chip pancake recipe that is super easy to make for a yummy chocolaty breakfast treat! Chocolate chip pancakes are a varition of the traditional pancakes, where the chocolate chips are incorperated into the batter and create melted chocolate surprises within the fluffy pancake!


Ingredients and Materials

    1 cup milk *
    1 large egg *
    cup all-purpose flour *
    2 tablespoons white sugar
    1 tablespoon baking powder
    1/4 teaspoon salt
    2 tablespoons butter (melted and cooled)
    1/2 cup mini chocolate chips
    * Allergens: dairy, egg, gluten

    Large skillet
    Bowl(s)
    Plate(s)
    Spatula

Instructions

    Step 1

    Gather ingredients

    Step 2

    Sift flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt into a separate bowl.

    Step 3

    Whisk milk and egg together in a bowl until thoroughly combined.

    Step 4

    Stir melted butter into the flour mixture, then whisk in milk mixture until smooth.

    Step 5

    Fold in chocolate chips.

    Step 6

    Heat a small nonstick skillet over low heat. Pour about 1/3 cup batter into the hot skillet and cover with a lid. Cook pancake until browned on the bottom, about 3 minutes.

    Step 7

    Flip pancake, then cover and cook until browned on the other side, 2 to 3 minutes. Remove to a plate and repeat to make remaining pancakes.

    Step 8

    Wrap extra pancakes in tinfoil and place in freezer safe ziplock bag. Pancakes can be safely stored in the freezer to be reheated at a later point.

Recipe Site Critiques

    delish
    I like this recipe website becasue it is very editorial, fun, and easy to navigate. The page has so many places to start or "jump off from" if you don't know where to start or are just looking to browse.
    Food Network
    Being such a recognized name, the Food Newtork website has an appeal to novice and advanced chefs. The site is easy to understand and pages are very accessible, listing all required ingredients, directions, etc clearly. Buttons make the page both engaging and interactive.
    HELLO FRESH
    I love the simple aesthetic and intuative organization of this website. Buttons are only placed where needed and all images are clickable and bring you to a new page. There is clear navigation at the top of the home screen to help sort and bring you where you want to go.

Non-Recipe Site Inspiration

    Patagonia
    While this is a more retial-oriented site, I love teh combination of text and images in this site. The large image grids are something I might be interested in exploring on my site. Food engages all of our senses, and large imagery might be important for conveying the taste or feeling of the recipe I chose without the ability to send smells or flavors through the screen.
    Noodles and Co.
    This website is probably one of my favorite all time websites, although I have never eaten there. The fun playful illustrations, animations, and imagery all come together so beautifully. I love the font choice and how it compliments the round shape of their images of pasta bowls, and the more curved and flowy incons and illustrations. They have a narrow color palette but it works for their branding.
    Pentagram
    Obviously Pentagram is a world-renowned design group, but their website is equally as stunning as their work. Not only do I love their visual design, their information design and orgnaization is equally as inspiring. They do a great job of balancing imagery, text, interactive, features, animation, color, and sound to communicate their work, but not in an overwhelming way. I hope to bring ideas of how to communicate information from their site to my recipe page.
